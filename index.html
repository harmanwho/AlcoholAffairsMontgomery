Introduction

Alcohol has maintained a large and crucial component in human affairs throughout history. On both social and economic scales, the use and distribution of alcohol are very influential forces. Thus, analyzing data relevant to the sale and any potential related violations can provide insights relevant to a location's economic and social affairs in the context of alcohol. In specific, using our various datasets that include information regarding the alcohol supply chain and the validity and reliability of alcohol sales over time, we can draw answers to the following example general questions:

  • How has the pandemic affected the sale of alcohol in Montgomery County?
  • Generally, which alcoholic beverages have the largest roles/presences in the special orders and sales within Montgomery?
  • Where do most alcohol violations take place, and are there any similarities between locations in regards to the nature of the violation?
  • How have the rate and nature of alcohol violations changed during the Covid era?
  • What potential trends can be found in Montgomery's history and response to alcohol violations?

Consumer markets can be incredibly complex. With a world full of wide varieties of different products and services, delivering variety as well as quality is the key to success when it comes to generating revenue. At the heart of this analysis, the goal is to gain insight into consumer behavior and what will lead to the most popular choices. This particular analysis will look at comparing the sales of different alcoholic beverages within montgomery county maryland.

  • While there is no correlation specifically betweenn Fulfilled Quantity and Fulfilled date, we do see small clusters form like on the 23rd where there were a lot of orders that took longer to order. This means that the backlog within the specific clusters might have more to do with the type of alcohol that is being made. We can see that with the hover, all of the dots that seem to be related to Wine.
  • This makes a lot of sense given that Montgomery County is one of the wealthiest counties in the united states, the larger wait times would make sense with more expensive types of wine. Traditionally, more expensive wines are also more aged, and thus ahrder to make. And if those wines are shipped from outside the US or sourced by complex methods, lots of proceedures to ensure the quality of the wine and avoiding damages to the bottles while shipping could be a priority that adds to the wait time.

Analysis of the kind of violations and their dispositions (penalties).

Amir Bagheri

Example Insight and Hypothesis

In Montgomery, certain kinds of violations occur more frequently than others, and violations of the same nature tend to have the same dispositions.

  • In this visualization, the 5 most frequently occuring violations are portrayed. To see the resulting penalty (disposition) to the offense, hover over the bars. Clearly, the sale of alcohol to minors is the most frequent violation, occuring more than twice the other following violations, at over 350 incidents. The next highest, also in a similar category of unlawful activity, is the lack of certified employees on the premises. The next 3 violations are all relatively common in that they are more so bureaucratic violations: less exciting infractions such as invoice violations, unavailable employee records, and unsatisfactory license displays.
  • As evident through the dispositions, selling alcohol to underage customers carries the heaviest penalty, also by a wide margin: The resulting disposition is a fine of $1000 in most cases, double the second highest fine listed. Interestingly, the records - related violations also seem to pack a hefty punch, with fines of $500 in the majority of instances.

Comparison of violations pre and post-Covid era.

Amir Bagheri

Example Insight and Hypothesis

The pandemic has had a measurable effect on the nature, frequency, and response to alcohol violations in Montgomery.

  • It is certainly a peculiar situation that Montgomery facilities seem to violate the most costly and serious enforcement most often; it seems as though the underage population of Montgomery can help themselves quite easily to a drink or two at these businesses. To get a better sense of what may be going on, this visualization focuses solely on sale-to-minor violations over time. Incidents listed after 2020 (post-covid) are represented with red.
  • The visualization provides multiple takeaways. Firstly, there seems to be a discernible wave-like trend in these violations over time: summer months tend to have far fewer instances, while autumn tends to begin with a sharp rise, decreasing over time until the summer. Secondly, unsurprisingly, covid-era months tend to have fewer incidents of underage sale, with large gaps in the summer and fall/winter months.

Side-by-side analysis of Alcohol Orders and their fulfillments

Harman Sidhu

Example Insight and Hypothesis

Alcohol types with the most number of Orders(Wine and Beer) have more consistent fulfillments as compared to Alcohol types with less number of Orders.

  • As observed in the charts below by clicking on 'Beer' and 'Wine' alcohol types, it is seen that although the orders are placed everyday consistently over the month, the fulfillments of the orders are accomplished starting Wednesdays and ending Sundays during the week.
  • Except for 'Beer' and 'Wine' the fulfillments are scattered all over the week from which it can be concluded that the suppliers tend to focus on more organized and timely deliveries for 'Wine' and 'Beer' orders even when the Order quantity is low because overall orders for the mentioned types of alcohol are high.
  • It can be said that there is improved and more stable delivery and fulfillment system established for certain types of alcohol given the overall demand for the mentioned.

Datasets

Datasets used in the analyses

Main Data

Department of Liquor Control Special Order Fulfillment - This dataset contains a log of last month's time taken to fulfill special orders in days. Update Frequency : Monthly

Licensee Data

Department of Alcohol Beverage Services (ABS) Licensee Data - List of all companies licensed by Montgomery County to sell alcoholic beverages to include name, address, and account number.

Violations Data

Alcoholic Beverage License Violations - The dataset includes alcohol violations as a result of sale to minor compliance checks, routine inspections and enforcement efforts. Update Frequency: Monthly
